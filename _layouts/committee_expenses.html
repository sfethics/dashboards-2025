The table below lists total expenses by local and state committees involved in the {{ page.election }} election. The total expense amount for each committee is based on transactions reported to the Ethics Commission within the two-year period prior to the election date. Click on any committee's link to view more details about that committee.

The table includes the following types of committees:

  • Candidate-Controlled Committee: A committee that is controlled by a candidate running in this election. Total expense for a candidate-controlled committee is based on transactions reported on Form 496 and Form 460 Schedule E.
  • Primarily Formed Candidate/Measure: A committee is “primarily formed” when it makes or initially plans to make more than 70% of its contributions and expenditures to support or oppose a specific candidate or measure, or a group of measures or specific local candidates all being voted upon in the same election on the same date. Total expense for a primarily formed committee is based on transactions reported on Form 496 and Form 460 Schedule E.
  • General Purpose Committee: A committee is “general purpose" if its principal activity is supporting or opposing a variety of candidates or measures voted on in different elections. Total expense for a general purpose committee is based on transactions reported on Form 496 and Form 460 Schedule D.
  • Independent Expenditure Committee: An individual or entity that makes independent expenditures from their own funds to support or oppose candidates or measures. Total expense for an independent expenditure committee is based on transactions reported on Form 461.
